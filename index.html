HISTORIC CENTER OF KRAKÓW

Cracow is home to Europe's largest Medieval market square, Church of the Holy Mary, Wawel Cathedral, Collegium Maius, Barbican, St Florian's Gate, Jewish quarter of Kazimierz, Jewish Culture Festival, Benedictine Abbey, and other cultural events..


OLD CITY OF ZAMOŚĆ

The historic center of Warsaw is an exception on UNESCO's World Cultural Heritage List due to its reconstruction after WWII destruction.



HISTORIC CENTRE OF WARSAW

Jan Sariusz Zamoyski, the Great Chancellor of the Crown, magnate and magistrate, was able to build Nowy Zamość with the help of Bernardo Mornado, an Italian architect who had been working in Poland since 1569..


CZOCHA CASTLE

The castle was designed as a fortress in the 13th century and restored to its former glory after centuries of political turbulence. Nowadays, it is home to an elegant hotel, Knights' tournaments, dance shows, comedy acts and concerts, as well as a stylish café and a restaurant specialising in Polish cuisine.


MAŁOPOLSKA PROVINCE

Małopolska Province is home to some of Poland's most visited sites.


ŁÓDŹ: CITY OF FILM AND FASHION

Łódź flourished during the 19th and 20th centuries due to investments and labor of Jews, Germans, Russians, and Poles.
